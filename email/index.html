Buoy Maintainance

The Climate Smart Pilots team will be down at the Clyde River from this Wednesday (4th May) to Friday (6th May) to conduct maintenance on the buoys and weather station. During this time an additional backup weather station will be installed to ensure accurate weather information is available at all times. Say hello to the team if you see them.

24 Hour Salinity and Temperature Viewer

A new map viewer has been added for both salinity and water temperature. Click the play button in the map tab to see the role that the ebb and flow of the tide has on the river over the past 24 hours.

Be aware this view requires a decent internet connection. As the malfunctioning buoys are brought back online the accuarcy of this map will increase.

Historical Water Temperature

Historical data containing monthly average, minimum and maximum water temperature from 1967 to 1973 has been added under the historical tab in the water temperature section. This data is compared to the previous three years 2020 to 2022, to describe overall changes in water temperature variation over the past 50 years.

From the chart it is expected that average water temperature on the Clyde River will decrease by roughly 2-3 °C in the month of May.
